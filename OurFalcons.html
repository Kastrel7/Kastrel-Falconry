Our Falcons

This is Kes, he is a common Kestrel. Kes is one of our most popular falcons in the falconry! Kestrel Falcons are also OUR favourite birds in the falconry and the origin of our name. Kestrel Falcons are very unique falcons in that they can hover in one place. Come visit Kes on a tour of Kastrel Falconry.

This is Peales, our native Peregrine Falcon. Peregrine Falcons are the fastest birds in the world with a maximum speed of 390km/hr when diving. Peales is definetily the fastest falcon in our falconry. She is also the oldest falcon in the falconry. If you want to see Peales ZOOM around the park, come visit on a Falcon Walk.

This is Earl, the Merlin Falcon. Earl is the youngest bird at our falconry! The Merlin Falcon is a small species of bird and is colloquially known as a pigeon hawk. Earl currently isn't old enough to hunt, but is able to perch on the arm and can be pet and fed. Come visit us today to take care of Earl!

This is Gyro, the Gyrfalcon. Gyrfalcons are the largest faclon species. The gyrfalcon is a very polymorphic species, so its plumage varies greatly. Our Gyro has beautiful white feathers that are really soft to the touch. Come down to the falconry today to see Gyro in action and stroke his beautiful, soft plumage.
